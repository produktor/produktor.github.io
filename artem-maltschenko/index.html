About

I’m Artem Maltschenko. I live and work in Nürnberg, and my day-to-day is split between ambulatory healthcare and data-sovereign digital infrastructure.

I run Edelweiss Ambulanter Pflegedienst GmbH as Geschäftsführer — home nursing, long-term care, and case management under Germany’s SGB V and SGB XI frameworks. In parallel, I build and operate self-hosted IT for care organisations: document systems, collaboration stacks, and searchable knowledge bases that keep sensitive data off foreign SaaS.

Background

My professional base is Nürnberg, Germany (Nordring 169, 90409). The Edelweiss Impressum lists me as managing director since October 2023, when Edelweiss was registered at Amtsgericht Nürnberg (HRB 42380).

Before Edelweiss, I served as Geschäftsführer of Rudi Ambulanter Pflegedienst GmbH (approx. 2020–September 2024) — same sector, same region. It was a change in structure, not a change in mission.

What drives the work

Two problems keep coming up in my work:

  1. Care quality at home — reliable nursing, 24-hour care options, and case management for families who need continuity, not bureaucracy.
  2. Digital sovereignty — care organisations should own their documents, calendars, and knowledge bases on infrastructure they control, aligned with GDPR and German healthcare practice.

The IT side is not a side hustle bolted onto a Pflegedienst. It’s the same conviction applied to software: if patient files and team communication live in foreign SaaS silos, you don’t truly control your data. Self-hosted stacks — OnlyOffice, WebDAV, S3-compatible storage, searchable archives — are the operational answer.
